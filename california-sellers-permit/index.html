Who needs a California seller’s permit?

California requires a seller’s permit for any individual or business that sells or leases tangible goods that would ordinarily be subject to sales tax — even part-time or online-only sellers operating from home.

You need one if you…

  • Sell online to California customers (Shopify, Amazon, Etsy, your own store)
  • Run a retail shop, boutique, or showroom
  • Sell wholesale to other businesses
  • Sell at pop-ups, fairs, swap meets, or farmers markets
  • Lease taxable equipment or goods

What you’ll need to apply

  • Legal business name and entity type
  • EIN (or SSN for sole proprietors)
  • Business address and contact details
  • What you plan to sell
  • About 5 minutes

California seller’s permit FAQ

How much does a California seller’s permit cost?

The state charges nothing — CDTFA registration is free. You pay our $75 flat service fee for guided preparation, expert error checking, and filing support. No hidden service fees, no subscription.

Do I need a permit to sell online from home?

Yes. If you sell taxable goods to California customers — even exclusively online through Shopify, Amazon, or Etsy — California requires a seller’s permit for your home-based business.

How long does it take?

The application takes about 5 minutes. Once filed, the CDTFA often issues permits the same day to within a few business days.

Does the permit expire or need renewal?

No — it stays valid while you actively operate. You’ll file sales tax returns on the schedule the CDTFA assigns (typically quarterly or annually).

What about temporary events like pop-ups?

California offers temporary seller’s permits (valid 90 days) for one-off events. If you sell regularly, a standard permit is usually the right choice — tell us about your business and we’ll prepare the appropriate application.
